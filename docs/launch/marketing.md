# Launch Marketing

## Goal

Prepare a minimal landing page, launch copy, and social assets for release.

## Marketing Site Structure

1. Landing page at `feasty.com`
   - Hero line
   - Three product benefits
   - Primary call to action
   - Footer links to privacy, terms, about, and contact
2. How it works page at `feasty.com/how-it-works`
   - Browse -> Add to cart -> Checkout -> Track delivery
3. About page at `feasty.com/about`
   - Short team bio and mission statement
4. Contact page at `feasty.com/contact`
   - Email form or support address

## Fastest Path

- Use a landing page builder such as Webflow, Framer, Carrd, or a simple hosted template.
- Or clone a Vercel template and customize it.

## Social Assets

1. X or Twitter launch post template
2. Instagram story carousel with 5 slides
3. Optional Facebook event
4. App store screenshots with short captions

## Launch Blog Post

Suggested title:

- Introducing FEASTy: Fast, Local Food Delivery

Suggested sections:

- Why FEASTy exists
- How it works
- Paystack checkout and real-time tracking
- Beta or launch call to action
- Contact details

## Optional Press Release

Use a short press release only if it fits the launch timeline. Revisit it after the first week of metrics if needed.

## Launch Checklist

- [ ] Landing page deployed to `feasty.com`
- [ ] Privacy policy linked in the footer
- [ ] Terms of service linked in the footer
- [ ] Launch blog post published
- [ ] Social post template ready
- [ ] Instagram carousel created
- [ ] App store screenshots prepared
- [ ] App store descriptions finalized
- [ ] Press release drafted, if needed
- [ ] Support email configured and monitored

## Suggested Timeline

- By day 25: landing page live
- By day 27: all assets ready to publish
- Day 28 to day 30: publish and announce
